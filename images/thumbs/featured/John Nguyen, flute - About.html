About

John-Baptist Nguyen is a college sophomore known for his passion and ambition for the flute. He has won the National Flute Association Collegiate Flute Choir Competition, Metropolitan Youth Symphony Concerto Competition, Rose City Wind Symphony Young Soloist Competition and received top prizes in the Greater Portland Flute Society Young Artists Competition and the Oregon State Solo Championships.

As a community leader, John owns and writes for Flute Watch, an online flute blog that serves hundreds of monthly readers. He also is the creator of the viral Carolyn Nussbaum What Flute Are You? filter, an Instagram camera feature that has accumulated over 65,000 downloads worldwide. In 2019, John founded and directed the Lavang Youth Chamber Orchestra, a student-led orchestra expanding musical opportunity for Vietnamese-Americans across the Northwest and served as the Chapter Director of Project Prelude, a non-profit teaching music lessons for disadvantaged youth.

Commended for his creative potential and community impact, John is currently one of the four finalists for the 2020 National Flute Association C.R.E.A.T.E. Competition at Washington DC. He also received awards including the Oregon Music Hall of Fame Scholarship, Beaverton Arts Foundation Scholarship, City of Beaverton Emerging Arts Opportunities Grant, Greater Portland Flute Society Scholarship, and the Westview Instrumental Music Department Award.

John is currently minoring in music at The University of Texas at Dallas on a full-tuition merit-based scholarship. John studies with flutist Won Lee; his past teachers include Dawn Weiss, Corrie Cook, Darren Cook and Sydney Carlson. He has performed for the masterclasses of world-renowned flutists including Julien Beaudiment, Ransom Wilson, Molly Barth, Marina Piccinini, Trudy Kane, and Jacqueline Cordova-Arrington. When he is not practicing, John collaborates with UT Dallas faculty to engineer solutions to the problems within the flute community and write articles for his blog.
